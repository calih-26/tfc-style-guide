## About the Collection
This guide organizes fashion aesthetics into keyword lists designed to support searching within the Texas Fashion Collection. Each aesthetic  is presented using a standardized format consisting of six sections: Scope Note, Color Terms, Material/Fabric Terms, Garment Types, Construction & Design Details, and Decades.

1. Scope Note
Goth fashion emerged from post-punk subculture in the late 1970s and early 1980s and incorporates visual references to Victorian mourning dress, romanticism, decadence, and alternative music scenes. The aesthetic is typically associated with dark color palettes (particularly black), textured fabrics such as velvet and lace, layered silhouettes, and historical revival elements.

Because “goth” is a contemporary and culturally specific term, it may not appear in formal archival descriptions. We encourage you to search using garment types, materials, construction details, decades, and historical references commonly associated with the aesthetic.

Combination searching is the best strategy. Here are some examples:
